About the Conference

Bringing together mathematicians, engineers, and scientists from across the globe.

ICAMM 2026

The International Conference on Applied Mathematics and Mechanics (ICAMM 2026) will be held at IIT Indore, India, during December 7-9, 2026. This conference is meant for researchers from academia, industries, technology and R&D organisations from all over the globe whose research is broadly related to applied mathematics and mechanics.

The conference puts special emphasis on the participation of PhD students, post-doctoral fellows and other young researchers from all over the world. The conference will feature world-class keynote speakers in the theme areas.

About IIT Indore

Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Indore, situated in Madhya Pradesh and founded in 2009 by the Government of India, is an institution of national importance. The verdant campus of IIT Indore, spread across 512 acres, is ranked among the top 50 universities in Asia.

The faculty members are well equipped to conduct high-quality research in both Science and Engineering, and are actively engaged in organizing various international research events. Visit iiti.ac.in →

Call for Proposals

Call for Minisymposia

Researchers are invited to organize focused thematic sessions within ICAMM 2026 on specialized topics in applied mathematics and mechanics.

What is a Minisymposium?

A minisymposium is a focused session of 90–120 minutes organized around a specific sub-topic by an expert researcher (the organizer). The organizer invites 4–8 speakers, chairs the session, and takes responsibility for its scientific content. It runs as a dedicated block in the conference program, giving a niche topic the depth it deserves.

How to Propose
01

Prepare a Proposal

Write a 200–400 word description of the theme, its relevance to ICAMM, and a tentative list of 4–8 invited speakers.

02

Submit to the Committee

Email your proposal to the organizing committee by 30 July 2026. Include organizer's name, affiliation, and contact.

03

Await Approval

The scientific committee will review and notify you of acceptance by 20 August 2026.

04

Speakers Submit Abstracts

Once approved, each speaker in your session submits their abstract individually through the main portal by 25 August 2026.

How to Reach IIT Indore

IIT Indore is located near Simrol village, ~25 km from Indore city on Khandwa Road, Madhya Pradesh.

  • ✈️
    By Air: Devi Ahilya Bai Holkar Airport (IDR), Indore – ~30 km from campus. Taxis available.
  • 🚆
    By Train: Indore Junction (INDB) – ~28 km. Auto-rickshaws and cabs to campus.
  • 🚌
    By Bus: Sarwate Bus Stand, Indore. Local buses toward Simrol / Khandwa Road.
  • 🚗
    By Road: Located on NH-52 (Indore–Khandwa Highway), approximately 25 km south of Indore city.
